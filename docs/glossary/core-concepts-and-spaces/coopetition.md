# Coopetition

## Summary

Coopetition is a strategy in which organizations cooperate in some areas while continuing to compete in others.

## Definition

The concept describes relationships where companies or ecosystem participants work together on shared goals such as innovation, standards, or infrastructure while still remaining market rivals. It requires a careful balance between collaboration and competitive protection.

## Why It Matters

Coopetition can accelerate learning, reduce duplicated effort, and open paths to market growth that no single player could create alone. In innovation ecosystems, it can also help organizations shift from zero-sum thinking toward shared-value creation.

## Industry Context

Examples of coopetition include shared research initiatives, joint ecosystem-building efforts, and collaboration on industry standards. These relationships work best when roles, governance, and boundaries are clear enough to preserve trust while managing competitive tension.
