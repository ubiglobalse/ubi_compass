# Ecosystem Orchestrator

## Summary

An ecosystem orchestrator is the person or organization that intentionally coordinates relationships, priorities, and collaboration across an innovation ecosystem.

## Definition

Orchestrators help align startups, investors, corporations, universities, policymakers, and other participants so they can work together productively. Their role is less about controlling every interaction and more about creating the structures and conditions that allow collective value creation to happen.

## Why It Matters

Innovation ecosystems depend on trust, coordination, and strategic alignment across many independent actors. An orchestrator helps reduce fragmentation, create shared momentum, and keep the ecosystem moving toward common goals.

## Industry Context

Orchestrators may use events, platforms, partnerships, governance arrangements, and strategic convening to help participants collaborate effectively. The role becomes more important as ecosystems grow more interconnected across sectors, institutions, and geographies.
