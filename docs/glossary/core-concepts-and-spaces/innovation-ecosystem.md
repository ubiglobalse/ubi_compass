# Innovation Ecosystem

## Summary

An innovation ecosystem is the interconnected network of organizations, people, institutions, and resources that helps new ideas become viable products, companies, and long-term economic activity.

## Definition

Innovation ecosystems bring together startups, incubators, accelerators, universities, investors, corporations, public institutions, and service providers in a shared environment. The value of the ecosystem comes from how these actors interact, collaborate, and reinforce one another over time.

## Why It Matters

Startups rarely succeed in isolation. A healthy ecosystem improves access to funding, expertise, infrastructure, partnerships, customers, and talent, making it easier for innovators to test ideas, learn quickly, and scale promising solutions.

## Industry Context

In the incubator and accelerator industry, the term is used to describe more than a local startup scene. It points to the full system that shapes innovation outcomes, including policy, research, capital, community leadership, and support organizations. Incubators and accelerators often act as connectors inside this system by linking founders to mentors, partners, and investment pathways.
