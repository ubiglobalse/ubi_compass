# Innovation Entrepreneurship Journey

## Summary

The innovation entrepreneurship journey describes the stages founders and startups move through as they turn an idea into a viable, scalable business.

## Definition

The journey usually includes phases such as ideation, validation, development, market entry, growth, and longer-term scale or exit. It reflects the fact that entrepreneurship is not a single moment but a progression through distinct challenges, decisions, and capability needs.

## Why It Matters

Understanding the journey helps programs, investors, and founders match the right support to the right stage. It also explains why startups need different tools, networks, and expectations as they move from early experimentation to more mature growth.

## Industry Context

Incubators, accelerators, and ecosystem enablers often organize their services around this journey, offering tailored support for each phase. The concept is especially useful when mapping how founders move from raw idea to commercialization, scaling, and eventual long-term impact.
