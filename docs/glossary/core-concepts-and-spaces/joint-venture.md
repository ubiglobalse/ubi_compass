# Joint Venture

## Summary

A joint venture is a formal business arrangement in which two or more organizations pool resources to pursue a specific shared objective while remaining independent entities.

## Definition

Participants in a joint venture agree on how they will share risk, control, responsibility, and potential return for a defined project or business activity. JVs are often used when the opportunity is too large, complex, or risky for one organization to pursue alone.

## Why It Matters

Joint ventures let organizations combine complementary assets, expertise, and market access in order to move faster or operate more effectively. In innovation contexts, they can support commercialization, market entry, and collaborative product development.

## Industry Context

Compared with looser partnership models, JVs usually involve more explicit contractual structure around governance and profit-sharing. They are especially relevant when organizations need deeper collaboration without giving up their separate identity.
