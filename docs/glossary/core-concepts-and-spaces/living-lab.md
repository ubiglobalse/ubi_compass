# Living Lab

## Summary

A living lab is a real-world innovation environment where users, companies, researchers, and public actors work together to co-create, test, and refine new solutions in authentic settings.

## Definition

Instead of developing ideas in isolation, a living lab brings experimentation into the environments where people actually use the product, service, or policy. This allows teams to combine prototyping with direct user feedback, practical testing, and collaborative iteration.

## Why It Matters

Living labs help organizations learn from real behavior rather than assumptions alone. That makes it easier to design solutions that are more relevant, more usable, and more likely to gain adoption in the environments they are meant to serve.

## Industry Context

Living labs are especially valuable in complex innovation settings where multiple stakeholders influence success, such as urban innovation, sustainability, health, mobility, and public-sector transformation. In startup ecosystems, they can help ventures move from early concept to more credible validation by grounding experimentation in real contexts.
