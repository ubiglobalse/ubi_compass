# Service Provider Network

## Summary

A service provider network is a coordinated group of specialists and professional firms that give startups access to legal, financial, technical, marketing, HR, and other critical support services.

## Definition

These networks connect founders and ecosystem participants with trusted external providers who can help build, operate, and scale a business. They may be organized as curated partner groups, program directories, or strategic support ecosystems attached to incubators, accelerators, or hubs.

## Why It Matters

Founders often need expert support they cannot afford or source quickly on their own. A strong service provider network reduces friction, improves access to trusted help, and strengthens the overall value proposition of a startup support program.

## Industry Context

Programs may negotiate preferred access, preferred pricing, or dedicated support through these networks. In mature ecosystems, service providers become part of the support infrastructure that helps founders stay focused on execution instead of repeatedly rebuilding back-office capability from scratch.
