# Startup Ecosystem

## Summary

A startup ecosystem is the network of founders, investors, institutions, support organizations, and shared resources that helps startups launch, grow, and survive within a specific region or sector.

## Definition

Startup ecosystems include startups at multiple stages as well as the actors that support them, such as incubators, accelerators, universities, corporations, government bodies, investors, and specialist service providers. What makes it an ecosystem is the interaction among these players, not just their presence in the same place.

## Why It Matters

The strength of a startup ecosystem shapes how easily founders can access capital, talent, partnerships, expertise, and community support. Healthy ecosystems reduce friction for entrepreneurs and improve the odds that promising ventures can move from idea to traction and from traction to scale.

## Industry Context

Strong startup ecosystems rely on both hard infrastructure, such as space, funding, and institutions, and soft infrastructure, such as trust, knowledge sharing, and founder culture. Incubators and accelerators often act as key nodes in the system because they help coordinate introductions, capability-building, and access to resources across the broader network.
