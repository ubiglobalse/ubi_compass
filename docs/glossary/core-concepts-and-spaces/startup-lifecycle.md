# Startup Lifecycle

## Summary

The startup lifecycle is the sequence of stages a startup moves through from idea formation to growth, maturity, and possible exit.

## Definition

Typical lifecycle stages include ideation, validation, product-market fit, scaling, maturity, and exit. Each stage comes with different operational needs, risk profiles, funding expectations, and support requirements.

## Why It Matters

The lifecycle framework helps founders and support organizations understand that startups need different types of help at different moments. It also helps benchmark programs design more targeted interventions instead of treating all ventures as if they are at the same stage.

## Industry Context

Lifecycle models vary somewhat by sector and geography, but they usually share the same broad progression from experimentation to growth. Incubators, accelerators, investors, and ecosystem partners often use lifecycle thinking to decide when to provide coaching, capital, commercialization support, or scale-up resources.
