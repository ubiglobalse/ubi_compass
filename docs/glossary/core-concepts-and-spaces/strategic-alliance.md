# Strategic Alliance

## Summary

A strategic alliance is a formal agreement between independent organizations that choose to collaborate toward shared strategic objectives without merging into a single entity.

## Definition

Strategic alliances let partners combine expertise, reach, or resources in order to create value that would be harder to achieve alone. Unlike mergers or acquisitions, alliances preserve organizational independence while enabling meaningful cooperation.

## Why It Matters

Alliances can help organizations enter new markets, reduce risk, share capability, and accelerate innovation. In startup ecosystems, they often create bridges between programs, institutions, corporations, and other actors that need to work together.

## Industry Context

Strategic alliances are usually more flexible and less structurally demanding than deeper integration models such as joint ventures or acquisitions. Their success depends on trust, aligned incentives, and clear rules for collaboration.
