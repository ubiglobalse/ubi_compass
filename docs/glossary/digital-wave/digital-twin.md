# Digital Twin

## Summary

A digital twin is a virtual representation of a product, system, or process that can be used for simulation, monitoring, and optimization.

## Definition

Digital twins mirror aspects of real-world objects or operations so teams can test scenarios, analyze behavior, and model changes before acting in the physical environment. They are often used in manufacturing, supply chains, product development, and infrastructure contexts.

## Why It Matters

Digital twins help reduce risk and speed up learning by giving organizations a safer environment for experimentation. For startups, they can improve decision-making, shorten development cycles, and support better forecasting around operations or product performance.

## Industry Context

As analytics, IoT, and AI capabilities improve, digital twins are becoming more useful in sectors where performance, maintenance, and scenario planning matter. Innovation programs may encounter them in deep-tech, industrial, mobility, health, and infrastructure ventures.
