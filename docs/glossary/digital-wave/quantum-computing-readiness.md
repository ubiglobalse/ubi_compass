# Quantum Computing Readiness

## Summary

Quantum computing readiness refers to how prepared an organization or startup is to understand, evaluate, and respond to emerging quantum technologies.

## Definition

Readiness includes building internal awareness, identifying relevant use cases, understanding security implications, developing partnerships, and deciding when experimentation is worth the effort. It does not necessarily mean actively building quantum products today.

## Why It Matters

Quantum technologies may affect computing, security, analytics, and long-term product strategy in fields where complex computation matters. Startups and support organizations that develop realistic readiness early may be better positioned to respond as the technology matures.

## Industry Context

For most programs, quantum readiness is still about literacy, governance, partnerships, and careful experimentation rather than immediate commercialization. It is especially relevant for ventures operating in security, advanced computing, simulation, and research-driven sectors.
