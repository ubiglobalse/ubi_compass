# DEI Monitoring

## Summary

DEI monitoring is the ongoing tracking of diversity, equity, and inclusion indicators within a program or among the startups it supports.

## Definition

This term measures whether a program regularly collects and reviews DEI-related data instead of relying on one-time snapshots or broad commitments. Monitoring can cover representation, participation, support access, and other inclusion-related signals over time.

## Why It Matters

Without monitoring, it is difficult to understand whether DEI efforts are improving outcomes or simply remaining aspirational. Continuous tracking helps programs identify gaps, refine support, and make more informed decisions about where intervention is needed.

## Industry Context

Useful DEI monitoring depends on strong data practices, appropriate privacy safeguards, and sensitivity to legal or cultural constraints. It is most effective when paired with a broader DEI strategy and a willingness to act on what the data shows.
