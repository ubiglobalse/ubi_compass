# DEI Stats

## Summary

DEI stats are the diversity, equity, and inclusion data points a program collects about founders, participants, or its broader startup community.

## Definition

In Compass, this term refers to the collection and use of DEI-related statistics to understand representation and inclusion patterns within a program or ecosystem. These figures provide a baseline for identifying who is being reached, who may be excluded, and where improvement is needed.

## Why It Matters

DEI statistics help transform inclusion from a general principle into something measurable. They can support targeted intervention, clearer reporting, and more informed decision-making about access, equity, and community impact.

## Industry Context

Collecting DEI data is valuable but not simple. Legal requirements, social norms, and privacy expectations differ by country, and some stakeholders may view these measures as sensitive or politically contested. Even so, robust DEI statistics remain a key foundation for credible social-impact work.
