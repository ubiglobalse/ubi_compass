# DEI Strategy

## Summary

DEI strategy refers to whether a program has a formal diversity, equity, and inclusion plan that is actively implemented rather than treated as a symbolic statement.

## Definition

In Compass, this term measures the extent to which a program has made DEI a structured organizational priority. That includes translating inclusive intent into policies, goals, practices, and operational decisions that shape how the program serves founders, staff, and stakeholders.

## Why It Matters

A credible DEI strategy helps institutionalize inclusive practice instead of leaving equity efforts to ad hoc decisions. It can improve program culture, broaden access, and create more equitable outcomes across recruitment, support services, partnerships, and measurement.

## Industry Context

DEI strategy overlaps with broader ESG work but keeps a specific focus on social equity and inclusion. Its effectiveness depends on more than written commitments. Real impact requires leadership backing, accountability, and enforcement strong enough to influence everyday program behavior.
