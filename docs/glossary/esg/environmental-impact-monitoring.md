# Environmental Impact Monitoring

## Summary

Environmental impact monitoring is the ongoing measurement of the ecological footprint of supported startups or program activity.

## Definition

This term asks whether a program tracks environmental effects in a structured way so it can understand and reduce ecological harm over time. Monitoring can cover emissions, resource use, waste, or other environmental indicators depending on the program context.

## Why It Matters

Programs cannot improve environmental performance reliably if they do not measure it. Monitoring creates the evidence base for better intervention, better reporting, and more credible sustainability commitments.

## Industry Context

Environmental monitoring can be technically demanding because metrics differ across sectors and high-quality data is not always easy to collect. Still, programs that monitor impact are better placed to validate environmental commitments and strengthen ESG management in practice.
