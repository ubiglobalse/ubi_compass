# Environmental, Social & Governance (SDG)

## Summary

ESG stands for environmental, social, and governance. It is a framework used to evaluate how organizations manage sustainability, social responsibility, and leadership or accountability practices.

## Definition

In Compass, ESG refers to the standards and operating practices that help incubators, accelerators, startups, and ecosystem organizations assess environmental impact, social outcomes, and governance quality. The framework is used to move beyond financial performance alone when judging organizational effectiveness and responsibility.

## Why It Matters

ESG helps organizations connect mission, risk management, operational discipline, and stakeholder expectations. For innovation programs, it also provides a language for understanding how startup support contributes to sustainability, inclusion, community benefit, and transparent leadership.

## Industry Context

ESG can include climate strategy, DEI work, governance processes, responsible funding decisions, and stakeholder engagement. UBI Global's 2021-2022 benchmark work also showed that ESG engagement was widespread among participating programs and was associated with stronger impact and performance signals without automatically reducing returns.
