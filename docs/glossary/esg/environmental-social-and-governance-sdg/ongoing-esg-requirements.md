# Ongoing ESG Requirements

## Summary

Ongoing ESG requirements are the environmental, social, and governance obligations a program expects client startups to follow after they have been admitted.

## Definition

This term measures whether ESG expectations continue beyond the admissions stage and become part of the startup's ongoing participation in the program. Requirements may involve reporting, policy adherence, training, or other forms of compliance and accountability.

## Why It Matters

When ESG expectations continue after admission, they are more likely to influence real operating behavior. Ongoing requirements help reinforce accountability, encourage continuous improvement, and make ESG part of how the program supports startup development over time.

## Industry Context

The effectiveness of ongoing requirements depends on how seriously they are enforced and how practical they are to monitor. Programs may need meaningful follow-up processes to ensure these expectations do more than exist on paper.
