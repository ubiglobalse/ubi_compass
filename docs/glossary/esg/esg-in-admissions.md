# ESG in Admissions

## Summary

ESG in admissions refers to whether environmental, social, and governance criteria influence which startups are accepted into a program.

## Definition

This term assesses whether ESG considerations are part of the selection process rather than only appearing later in program delivery. Admissions criteria may look at responsible business practices, impact orientation, governance maturity, or alignment with broader sustainability goals.

## Why It Matters

Including ESG in admissions helps shape the startup portfolio from the start. It signals that responsible entrepreneurship matters early and can align program intake with the values or impact goals the organization wants to advance.

## Industry Context

Programs vary widely in how deeply they apply ESG at admission. For some, it is a meaningful selection lens. For others, it may be a light-touch consideration or a flexible discussion point rather than a hard threshold. The usefulness of the metric depends on how consistently the criteria are applied.
