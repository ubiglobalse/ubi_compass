# ESG in Funding

## Summary

ESG in funding refers to whether environmental, social, and governance criteria affect how a program allocates capital to client startups.

## Definition

This term measures whether funding decisions are shaped by ESG expectations, impact orientation, or sustainability-related standards. The metric can apply to programs that screen for ESG alignment, prioritize impact-led ventures, or use ESG as one consideration among many.

## Why It Matters

Funding decisions strongly influence which startups grow and what kinds of ventures receive support. When ESG criteria shape funding, programs can direct resources toward businesses that are more aligned with responsible or impact-focused outcomes.

## Industry Context

The depth of ESG influence in funding varies widely. Some programs use ESG as a light screening factor, while others make it central to investment decisions. Because ESG definitions are not always standardized, the credibility of this metric depends on how clearly the criteria are defined and applied.
