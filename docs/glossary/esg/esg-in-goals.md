# ESG in Goals

## Summary

ESG in goals means environmental, social, and governance principles are embedded in a program's mission, strategy, or stated objectives.

## Definition

This term measures whether ESG is part of the philosophical and strategic core of the organization rather than a secondary or isolated initiative. It looks at whether the program explicitly connects its purpose and long-term direction to sustainability, responsibility, and wider stakeholder impact.

## Why It Matters

When ESG is built into organizational goals, it can shape decision-making, resource allocation, and program behavior more consistently. It sets expectations for what the organization values and can make later ESG activity feel more credible.

## Industry Context

Strategic alignment is important, but goals alone do not guarantee execution. Programs can still fall into performative or vague ESG positioning if those goals are not backed by practical implementation, measurement, and accountability.
