# ESG Strategy

## Summary

ESG strategy refers to whether a program has an actionable environmental, social, and governance plan that is embedded in day-to-day operations rather than treated as a stand-alone aspiration.

## Definition

In Compass, this term measures whether ESG goals are translated into practical program activity and long-term planning. A strong ESG strategy does not stop at statements of intent. It shapes how a program sets priorities, allocates resources, and evaluates progress.

## Why It Matters

An operational ESG strategy helps a program move from high-level commitments to measurable action. It creates consistency, improves accountability, and makes it easier to connect ESG goals with admissions, support services, partnerships, reporting, and broader organizational decision-making.

## Industry Context

Programs increasingly face pressure from stakeholders to show that ESG priorities are not only visible but executable. That said, a written strategy alone is not enough. Real impact depends on leadership support, resourcing, follow-through, and alignment with other organizational functions.
