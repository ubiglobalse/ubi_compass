# ESG Systematically Assessed

## Summary

ESG systematically assessed means a program has formal processes for regularly measuring and reviewing its environmental, social, and governance performance.

## Definition

This term asks whether ESG criteria are evaluated in a structured, repeatable way across the organization. The focus is not just on having ESG goals, but on checking progress against them through real assessment activity.

## Why It Matters

Systematic assessment helps programs move beyond rhetoric and understand whether ESG commitments are producing meaningful results. It supports accountability, gives leaders a basis for course correction, and makes it easier to improve policy and operations over time.

## Industry Context

Programs that assess ESG systematically are usually better positioned to report progress, identify gaps, and adapt their practices. At the same time, assessment requires time, resources, and staff capability. Measurement alone is not enough if the findings do not lead to action.
