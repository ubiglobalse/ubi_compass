# Bootstrapping

## Summary

Bootstrapping is the practice of starting and growing a business using personal funds, early revenue, or other self-generated resources rather than outside investment.

## Definition

Bootstrapped startups usually operate lean, reinvest revenue carefully, and make disciplined choices about spending, hiring, and growth. The approach often relies on founder savings, customer payments, and resourcefulness rather than institutional capital.

## Why It Matters

Bootstrapping can preserve ownership, force financial discipline, and keep founders closely connected to customer demand. It also shapes how incubators and advisors support a startup, because the company may prioritize cash flow, sustainable growth, and low-cost execution over aggressive fundraising.

## Industry Context

Some founders bootstrap by choice to retain control, while others do it because external capital is unavailable or inappropriate at their stage. Programs can help by teaching lean operations, strengthening early revenue generation, and connecting founders with practical support that reduces cash burn.
