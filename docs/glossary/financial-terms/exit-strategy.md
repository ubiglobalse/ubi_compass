# Exit Strategy

## Summary

An exit strategy is the plan founders, owners, or investors use to transfer ownership, realize returns, or transition leadership when a company reaches certain goals or conditions.

## Definition

Exit strategies can include paths such as acquisition, IPO, buyout, or liquidation. They help stakeholders think in advance about timing, valuation, governance, and the practical steps needed to leave or transform their relationship to the company.

## Why It Matters

Planning for exit shapes strategic decisions long before the event itself. It helps founders and investors align expectations, manage risk, and prepare the company for smoother transitions when growth, market conditions, or ownership goals change.

## Industry Context

Exit strategy is relevant to governance, fundraising, founder alignment, and long-term business planning. A well-considered exit path can make a startup more attractive to investors because it shows that the team understands both growth and eventual liquidity scenarios.
