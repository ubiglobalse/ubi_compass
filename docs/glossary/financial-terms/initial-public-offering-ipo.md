# Initial Public Offering (IPO)

## Summary

An initial public offering, or IPO, is the first time a privately held company offers shares to public investors on a stock exchange.

## Definition

Through an IPO, a company moves from private ownership into public-market participation, usually to raise capital, increase visibility, and create liquidity for founders and earlier investors. The process typically involves underwriters, regulatory filings, pricing decisions, and investor roadshows.

## Why It Matters

An IPO is one of the most visible exit and growth milestones a startup can reach. It can provide large-scale capital and a new level of market credibility, but it also brings added governance, disclosure, and performance expectations.

## Industry Context

For most startups, IPO readiness is a late-stage consideration tied to scale, operational maturity, and market timing. Incubators and accelerators are more likely to help indirectly by building earlier-stage governance, fundraising, and investor-relations capability long before a public listing becomes realistic.
