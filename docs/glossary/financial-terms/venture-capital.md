# Venture Capital

## Summary

Venture capital is equity financing provided to high-growth startups and emerging companies that are too risky or too early for many traditional lenders.

## Definition

Venture capital firms invest money in exchange for ownership in a company, usually with the expectation that the business can grow rapidly and generate significant returns. These investors often back companies that have strong growth potential but limited operating history or limited access to bank financing.

## Why It Matters

Venture capital can provide the funding needed for hiring, product development, market expansion, and operational scale. In the incubator and accelerator world, VC also acts as an important signal of market confidence and a pathway toward larger follow-on funding rounds.

## Industry Context

VC investors typically contribute more than capital. They may also offer strategic guidance, governance participation, introductions to customers or later-stage investors, and help with investment readiness. Many incubators and accelerators prepare startups to engage with venture capital by strengthening traction, storytelling, financial planning, and growth discipline before a fundraise.
