# Commercialization Program

## Summary

A commercialization program is a structured initiative that helps research teams, founders, or startups bring new technologies and innovations to market.

## Definition

These programs support activities such as licensing, pilot partnerships, customer validation, and early market entry. Compared with traditional accelerators, they are often less focused on equity investment and more focused on translating promising innovation into usable commercial outcomes.

## Why It Matters

Commercialization programs help bridge the gap between invention and adoption. They can reduce friction around market validation, industry access, and first-customer engagement, especially for research-driven or deep-tech ventures.

## Industry Context

They often provide legal guidance, sector expertise, partnership access, and commercialization pathways without requiring founders to give up the same level of ownership or control that more investment-led programs may request.
