# Globalization

## Summary

Globalization in the startup context refers to the spread of innovation activity across countries and regions, along with the growing importance of cross-border networks, markets, and capital.

## Definition

The term describes how startups, investors, and support organizations increasingly operate beyond a single local hub. Incubators and accelerators often help founders navigate this reality by building international partnerships, mentor networks, and market-access pathways that support global growth.

## Why It Matters

Globalization gives startups access to wider customer bases, broader funding opportunities, and more diverse sources of expertise. It also means founders need stronger cross-border capability in areas such as market entry, partnership development, and cultural navigation.

## Industry Context

As startup ecosystems mature around the world, innovation is no longer concentrated only in a few traditional hubs. Programs that can help founders access international markets and relationships often create stronger long-term growth opportunities for the ventures they support.
