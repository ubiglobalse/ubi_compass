# Growth Stage

## Summary

Growth stage is the phase in which a startup has achieved meaningful product-market traction and is focused on scaling operations, customers, revenue, and organizational capacity.

## Definition

At this stage, the company usually moves beyond early validation and starts investing more heavily in sales, marketing, hiring, systems, and market expansion. Growth-stage startups are typically managing more complexity than early-stage ventures and often require larger rounds of capital.

## Why It Matters

Growth stage is when many startups shift from proving they can work to proving they can scale. The risks change from pure idea validation to execution, operational efficiency, and maintaining quality while expanding rapidly.

## Industry Context

Programs and investors supporting this phase often focus on scaling systems, leadership maturity, customer growth, and fundraising readiness. Growth-stage companies may also begin entering new geographies or market segments as they expand.
