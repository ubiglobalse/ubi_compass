# Intellectual Property (IP)

## Summary

Intellectual property, or IP, refers to legally protected intangible assets such as inventions, designs, creative works, symbols, and proprietary knowledge.

## Definition

IP protection can take forms such as patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, and industrial designs. These rights help founders and organizations control how their innovations are used, monetized, and defended.

## Why It Matters

For startups, IP can influence valuation, competitive advantage, fundraising, licensing potential, and long-term strategic control. It is especially important when innovation depends on novel technology, brand differentiation, or research commercialization.

## Industry Context

IP management sits at the center of commercialization, technology transfer, licensing, and investor readiness. It also continues to evolve as new issues emerge around AI, inventorship, ownership, and the boundaries of protected creative or technical work.
