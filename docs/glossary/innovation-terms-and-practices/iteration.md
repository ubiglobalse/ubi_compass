# Iteration

## Summary

Iteration is the repeated cycle of building, testing, learning, and refining a product, service, or strategy based on feedback and evidence.

## Definition

Instead of aiming for a perfect solution immediately, teams improve through successive rounds of change. Each cycle generates information that helps founders decide what to keep, modify, or discard.

## Why It Matters

Iteration reduces risk by encouraging learning before major commitments are made. It helps startups adapt faster, improve user fit, and avoid investing heavily in assumptions that have not yet been validated.

## Industry Context

Iteration is central to modern startup methods such as lean experimentation, MVP development, and customer-driven product refinement. Incubators often reinforce iteration by encouraging testing, learning, and adaptive decision-making over rigid planning.
