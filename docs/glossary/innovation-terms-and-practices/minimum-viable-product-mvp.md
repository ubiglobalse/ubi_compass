# Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

## Summary

A minimum viable product, or MVP, is the simplest version of a product that delivers enough value to test core assumptions with real users.

## Definition

The MVP is not a final product. It is an intentionally limited release designed to generate validated learning quickly and at lower cost than a full build. The focus is on learning what users actually need before expanding scope.

## Why It Matters

MVPs help startups reduce waste, accelerate experimentation, and make better decisions about what to build next. They are especially useful when teams need evidence of demand, usability, or value before committing significant time and capital.

## Industry Context

Incubators and accelerators often help founders define MVP scope, gather early user feedback, and run iteration cycles that turn a rough concept into something increasingly market-ready. MVP thinking is central to lean startup practice and product validation.
