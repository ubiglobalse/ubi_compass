# Open Innovation

## Summary

Open innovation is the practice of using ideas, technologies, and expertise from both inside and outside an organization to drive innovation faster and more effectively.

## Definition

Instead of relying only on internal R&D, open innovation encourages organizations to collaborate with startups, customers, research institutions, partners, and sometimes even competitors. It treats innovation as a networked activity rather than a closed internal process.

## Why It Matters

Open innovation can accelerate development, increase access to expertise, and create stronger paths between emerging ideas and practical adoption. It is especially relevant where no single actor has all the knowledge, capabilities, or market access needed to succeed alone.

## Industry Context

Common open-innovation activities include co-creation, corporate-startup partnerships, technology scouting, shared pilots, and challenge-based collaboration. Incubators often play a useful role by connecting startups with larger organizations that need external innovation input.
