# Pivot

## Summary

A pivot is a deliberate change in a startup's strategy made in response to evidence that the current direction is not the best path forward.

## Definition

The shift can involve the product, target market, business model, pricing, channel strategy, or another core element of the venture. A pivot is not random change. It is meant to be a structured response to learning from the market or from performance data.

## Why It Matters

Pivots help startups stay adaptable without abandoning discipline. They allow teams to respond to real feedback and improve product-market alignment rather than doubling down on a weak strategy.

## Industry Context

Programs often help founders interpret the signals that justify a pivot and decide how large the change should be. Good pivoting relies on evidence, not panic, and it usually works best when teams preserve learning while redirecting execution.
