# Product-Market Fit

## Summary

Product-market fit is the point at which a product meets the needs of a well-defined market strongly enough to create real demand and sustained customer pull.

## Definition

It describes the alignment between what a startup offers and what a specific group of users actually wants or values. Achieving product-market fit usually means the startup has moved beyond pure experimentation and is seeing evidence of strong adoption or retention.

## Why It Matters

Product-market fit is one of the clearest signals that a startup may be ready to scale. Without it, growth efforts often amplify inefficiency; with it, the company has a stronger basis for expansion, hiring, and larger fundraising.

## Industry Context

Teams usually reach product-market fit through repeated cycles of customer feedback, iteration, and refinement. Incubators and accelerators often support this process through user research, market validation, and structured experimentation.
