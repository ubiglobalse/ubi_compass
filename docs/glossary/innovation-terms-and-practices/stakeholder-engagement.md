# Stakeholder Engagement

## Summary

Stakeholder engagement refers to the formal involvement of relevant external or internal stakeholders in program decision-making, governance, or accountability processes.

## Definition

In Compass, the term measures whether a program creates structured ways for ecosystem actors to influence policies, provide feedback, or help shape accountability. Stakeholders may include founders, partners, funders, community representatives, or other groups affected by program decisions.

## Why It Matters

Meaningful engagement can improve transparency, broaden perspective, and strengthen trust between a program and the ecosystem around it. It also helps reduce the risk that decisions are made without input from the people most affected by them.

## Industry Context

Stakeholder engagement is often discussed in ESG and governance contexts because it relates to openness, legitimacy, and accountability. Still, engagement only creates value when it is substantive enough to influence decisions rather than existing as a symbolic exercise.
