# Stealth Mode

## Summary

Stealth mode is a temporary period in which a startup operates with limited public visibility in order to protect its ideas, product direction, or strategic position.

## Definition

During stealth mode, founders usually restrict what they share publicly about the company, often using NDAs, internal codenames, limited marketing, and selective communication with outsiders. The goal is to reduce exposure while the team develops or tests something sensitive.

## Why It Matters

Stealth mode can protect intellectual property, reduce competitive pressure, and give teams space to refine a product before public scrutiny increases. It can be especially useful when timing, secrecy, or technical defensibility matter.

## Industry Context

Operating in stealth can also create tradeoffs. It may make recruiting, fundraising, customer discovery, or early brand-building harder. Programs advising stealth-mode startups often need to help founders decide when secrecy is worth the cost and when more visibility would accelerate learning.
