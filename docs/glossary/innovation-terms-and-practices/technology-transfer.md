# Technology Transfer

## Summary

Technology transfer is the process of moving inventions, research findings, or scientific discoveries from labs and institutions into real-world products, services, or companies.

## Definition

The process usually involves protecting intellectual property, assessing commercial potential, negotiating licenses or partnership terms, and creating pathways for research to reach the market. It often connects universities, research institutions, founders, investors, and industry partners.

## Why It Matters

Without effective technology transfer, valuable research may never create practical or economic impact. In startup ecosystems, it is a critical bridge between invention and commercialization, helping promising ideas become scalable ventures or adopted technologies.

## Industry Context

Technology transfer professionals often work across legal, financial, and operational questions at the same time. The work can involve licensing agreements, startup formation, investor readiness, and collaboration with corporate or public-sector partners that help turn early innovation into market-ready outcomes.
