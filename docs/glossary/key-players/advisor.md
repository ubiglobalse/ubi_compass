# Advisor

## Summary

An advisor is a specialist who helps founders solve specific business problems by contributing focused expertise, judgment, and connections in areas such as finance, marketing, legal, technology, or operations.

## Definition

Advisors usually work with startups on a targeted or issue-specific basis rather than through a long-term developmental relationship. Their value often comes from domain depth, pattern recognition, and the ability to guide founders through unfamiliar strategic or operational decisions.

## Why It Matters

Early-stage teams rarely have every critical skill in-house. Advisors help fill knowledge gaps, shorten learning curves, and add credibility in front of investors, partners, or customers. In incubator and accelerator environments, they also expand the support network available to founders beyond the core program staff.

## Industry Context

Advisors may participate in board discussions, fundraising preparation, commercial strategy, or specialist problem-solving sessions. Unlike mentors, who often build broad and longer-term relationships, or coaches, who focus on structured growth and accountability, advisors tend to be brought in for precise expertise tied to a business need.
