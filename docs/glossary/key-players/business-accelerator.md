# Business Accelerator

## Summary

A business accelerator is a fixed-term, cohort-based program that helps selected startups grow quickly through intensive support, mentorship, and access to investors, partners, and market opportunities.

## Definition

In the startup support context, accelerators typically work with ventures that are beyond the earliest idea stage and need help sharpening traction, investment readiness, and scaling capability. Programs are usually time-bound, highly selective, and structured around a shared cohort experience that ends in a showcase, graduation event, or demo day.

## Why It Matters

Accelerators concentrate expertise, peer learning, and network access into a short period of time. That makes them valuable for founders who need rapid feedback, market exposure, and faster progress toward fundraising, customer growth, or operational maturity.

## Industry Context

UBI Global, InBIA, European Commission, and academic definitions all emphasize the same core pattern: accelerators provide structured business support, often with coaching, mentoring, and investor exposure, within an intentionally compressed timeline. Some definitions also extend the term to programs that accelerate the growth of existing small businesses, but in Compass the primary meaning centers on startup-focused cohort programs.
