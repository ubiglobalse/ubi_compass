# Ecosystem Builder

## Summary

An ecosystem builder is a person or organization that actively develops the relationships, infrastructure, and shared momentum that help an innovation ecosystem grow stronger over time.

## Definition

Ecosystem builders connect startups, investors, universities, corporations, public institutions, and support organizations in ways that make collaboration easier and more productive. Their work often includes convening communities, creating partnerships, telling ecosystem stories, and strengthening shared identity.

## Why It Matters

Healthy ecosystems rarely emerge on their own. Builders help create the trust, visibility, and connectivity that allow other actors to succeed together. Their work can improve resource access, increase entrepreneurial activity, and strengthen resilience across the broader system.

## Industry Context

Ecosystem builders often operate as neutral connectors rather than owners of a single part of the ecosystem. They may run events, organize networks, advocate for missing support, or help different communities work together more effectively across sectors and institutions.
