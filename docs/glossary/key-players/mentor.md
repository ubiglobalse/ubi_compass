# Mentor

## Summary

A mentor is an experienced professional who supports founders over time with guidance, perspective, and relationship-based advice that goes beyond a short-term tactical intervention.

## Definition

Mentors help startup founders by sharing industry knowledge, lived experience, and strategic judgment. Unlike more structured support roles, mentorship usually develops as an ongoing relationship that can continue across multiple stages of the business journey.

## Why It Matters

Strong mentors can help founders avoid common mistakes, build confidence, and make better strategic decisions under uncertainty. In incubator and accelerator settings, mentorship also expands a startup's access to networks, trusted introductions, and hard-won practical insight.

## Industry Context

Mentorship is most effective when the relationship is built on trust, relevant experience, and open communication. Mentors often know when to challenge assumptions, when to encourage persistence, and when to help a founder reframe a problem. In practice, startups may rely on mentors, advisors, and coaches at the same time, but each role serves a different purpose.
