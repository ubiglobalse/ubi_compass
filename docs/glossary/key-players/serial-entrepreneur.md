# Serial Entrepreneur

## Summary

A serial entrepreneur is a founder who starts and leads multiple ventures over the course of a career rather than building only one company.

## Definition

These founders repeatedly create, grow, and sometimes exit startups, using lessons and networks from earlier ventures to shape later ones. Their experience often gives them stronger intuition about risk, timing, and execution.

## Why It Matters

Serial entrepreneurs contribute more than new companies. They often strengthen ecosystems by mentoring newer founders, reusing knowledge across ventures, and increasing the flow of experience, capital, and relationships within startup communities.

## Industry Context

Because they have already been through parts of the startup lifecycle, serial entrepreneurs are often seen as valuable guides, collaborators, or investors. Their resilience and pattern recognition can make them influential actors in both venture creation and ecosystem development.
