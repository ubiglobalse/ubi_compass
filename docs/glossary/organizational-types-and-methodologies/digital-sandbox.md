# Digital Sandbox

## Summary

A digital sandbox is a controlled environment where startups can test new digital products, services, or technologies safely before full-scale launch.

## Definition

Digital sandboxes let teams experiment with features, workflows, or technical systems under realistic conditions without exposing the full business, customer base, or regulatory environment to unnecessary risk. They are often used to validate assumptions, test compliance-sensitive products, and refine solutions before wider deployment.

## Why It Matters

This approach helps founders and support organizations reduce risk while accelerating learning. Instead of waiting for a full rollout, startups can gather feedback, identify technical or operational issues early, and improve the product before investing in broader expansion.

## Industry Context

Digital sandboxes became especially visible in regulated sectors such as fintech, but the model now appears across AI, telecom, climate tech, and public innovation programs. For incubators and accelerators, a sandbox can be an important bridge between early product work and market entry because it supports experimentation, stakeholder feedback, and safer testing conditions.
