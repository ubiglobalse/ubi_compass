# Dual-Track Incubation

## Summary

Dual-track incubation describes an incubation model that supports startups through both in-person and virtual participation.

## Definition

In a dual-track model, founders can access program resources, mentorship, events, and support through physical spaces as well as digital channels. This gives incubators flexibility in how they serve participants and broadens access for entrepreneurs who may be constrained by geography, travel, or scheduling.

## Why It Matters

This structure makes incubation more inclusive and adaptable. It allows programs to reach a wider founder base while still preserving the relationship-building, collaboration, and resource access that make incubation valuable.

## Industry Context

Dual-track models became more common as startup support organizations expanded their digital delivery capabilities. For incubators, the real value is not simply offering online access, but designing the program so remote and in-person founders can both participate meaningfully in mentoring, peer learning, and ecosystem connection.
