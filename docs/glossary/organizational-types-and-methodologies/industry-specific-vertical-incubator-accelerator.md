# Industry-Specific (Vertical) Incubator/Accelerator

## Summary

An industry-specific, or vertical, incubator or accelerator is a startup support program focused on ventures within a particular sector such as fintech, healthtech, cleantech, biotech, or edtech.

## Definition

Unlike generalist programs, vertical incubators and accelerators concentrate their support around the realities of one industry. That usually includes specialized mentors, relevant investors, domain knowledge, regulatory guidance, and corporate or institutional connections that are especially useful for companies in that field.

## Why It Matters

Sector focus can help startups move faster because they receive advice and introductions that are directly tied to their market. For founders working in complex or highly regulated areas, this specialization can reduce avoidable mistakes and improve their chances of gaining traction.

## Industry Context

Vertical programs often emerge where a region or institution has deep expertise in a specific field. They are commonly linked to universities, hospitals, corporations, labs, or industry clusters. In the broader support landscape, they complement generalist incubators and accelerators by serving founders whose needs require deeper technical or market-specific support.
