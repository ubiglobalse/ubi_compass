# Innovation Hub

## Summary

An innovation hub is a physical or virtual environment where startups, companies, researchers, investors, and other ecosystem actors come together to exchange ideas, access support, and collaborate on new solutions.

## Definition

Innovation hubs are designed to concentrate people, knowledge, and opportunities in one place. They often host events, workshops, mentoring, partnerships, and shared resources that help participants explore new ideas, strengthen networks, and move innovations forward.

## Why It Matters

These hubs reduce the isolation that many founders and innovators face by creating a space where expertise, introductions, and collaboration are easier to access. For early-stage ventures, that can accelerate learning, partnership formation, and market progress.

## Industry Context

Innovation hubs can sit inside research parks, urban innovation districts, universities, corporate environments, or digital communities. In the broader startup support landscape, they often function as connective tissue across an ecosystem by linking entrepreneurs with institutions, talent, capital, and sector-specific insight.
