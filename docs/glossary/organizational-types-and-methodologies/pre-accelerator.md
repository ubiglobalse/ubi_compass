# Pre-Accelerator

## Summary

A pre-accelerator is a short, early-stage program that helps founders sharpen an idea, validate a problem, and build the basic skills needed before joining a more intensive incubator or accelerator.

## Definition

Pre-accelerators usually work with very early-stage entrepreneurs who are still testing customer needs, refining a value proposition, or learning the fundamentals of startup development. The format is often workshop-driven and time-bound, with a focus on readiness rather than rapid scale.

## Why It Matters

Not every startup is ready for a full accelerator or longer incubation program. Pre-accelerators help founders build a stronger foundation first, which can improve later program fit, increase acceptance into competitive cohorts, and make investor or partner conversations more credible.

## Industry Context

These programs often include mentor-led sessions on customer discovery, market research, pitch development, and early business-model validation. Compared with accelerators, they are usually shorter, less capital-focused, and less likely to require equity. Their main role is to move founders from raw concept toward informed readiness.
