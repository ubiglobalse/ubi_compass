# Research and Technology Park

## Summary

A research and technology park is a property-based innovation environment, often connected to a university, research institution, or government body, that supports knowledge-based companies through space, infrastructure, and proximity to research activity.

## Definition

These parks typically host startups, established firms, labs, and support organizations in one shared environment. Unlike incubators or accelerators, they do not always provide structured venture programming, but they create conditions that make technology transfer, collaboration, and commercialization more likely.

## Why It Matters

Research and technology parks help turn institutional knowledge into economic activity. They give startups access to facilities, networks, and partnerships that might otherwise be difficult to reach, especially in sectors where research capability and technical infrastructure matter.

## Industry Context

In innovation ecosystems, these parks often act as long-term anchors rather than short-cycle programs. Their value comes from concentration: researchers, startups, corporations, and public actors are close enough to collaborate more easily, which can strengthen regional innovation capacity and support venture formation over time.
