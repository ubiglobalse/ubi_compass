# Venture Builder (Startup Studio)

## Summary

A venture builder, also called a startup studio, is an organization that systematically creates and launches new ventures by supplying capital, talent, and operational support from within the organization itself.

## Definition

Instead of only advising or investing in existing startups, venture builders often originate ideas internally, assemble teams, fund early development, and provide shared services across product, strategy, hiring, and operations. They usually hold equity in the ventures they help create.

## Why It Matters

This model gives early-stage companies more hands-on support than many traditional startup support structures. It can reduce execution friction, especially when a new venture needs coordinated help across multiple functions before it can stand on its own.

## Industry Context

Venture builders sit somewhere between founders, operators, and investors. In the startup support landscape, they differ from incubators and accelerators because they are more directly involved in venture creation and execution. The model is especially useful for ambitious opportunities that benefit from tight collaboration, rapid iteration, and repeatable venture-creation infrastructure.
