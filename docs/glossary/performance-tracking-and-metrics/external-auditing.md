# External Auditing

## Summary

External auditing refers to the use of independent third-party reviews to assess an organization's ESG or governance practices.

## Definition

In this context, auditing means bringing in an outside body to evaluate whether reported practices, controls, or performance claims are credible and aligned with recognized standards. These reviews can cover governance, environmental practices, social commitments, or other accountability areas.

## Why It Matters

Third-party verification can strengthen trust with stakeholders by showing that important claims are not based only on self-reporting. It can also surface gaps that help organizations improve their systems, reporting quality, and internal discipline over time.

## Industry Context

For incubators, accelerators, and related programs, external auditing is often associated with stronger governance maturity. At the same time, it can be resource-intensive, which means not every organization will pursue formal audits at the same stage. Its usefulness depends on both the quality of the audit and the organization's readiness to act on the findings.
