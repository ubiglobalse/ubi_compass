# Net Promoter Score (NPS)

## Summary

Net Promoter Score, or NPS, is a customer loyalty metric that measures how likely someone is to recommend a company, product, or service to others.

## Definition

It is usually based on a survey question that asks respondents to rate their likelihood of recommending the offering on a scale from 0 to 10. Responses are grouped into promoters, passives, and detractors, and the score is calculated by subtracting the percentage of detractors from the percentage of promoters.

## Why It Matters

NPS offers a simple way to track customer sentiment and advocacy. For organizations supporting startups, it can be a useful shorthand for understanding whether a product or service is creating positive user experiences strong enough to drive retention, referrals, and growth.

## Industry Context

Because it is easy to communicate and benchmark, NPS is widely used across industries as one signal of customer experience quality. It should not stand alone, but when interpreted alongside retention, usage, and qualitative feedback, it can help teams judge whether customers are genuinely enthusiastic or only minimally satisfied.
