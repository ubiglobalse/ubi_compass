# Technology Readiness Level

## Summary

Technology Readiness Level, or TRL, is a scale used to describe how mature a technology is, from early concept through real-world operational use.

## Definition

The framework typically uses nine levels, beginning with basic principles and early proof, then progressing through validation, prototyping, testing, and full deployment. It gives teams and stakeholders a structured way to describe where a technology currently stands and what kind of risk remains.

## Why It Matters

TRL helps innovators, investors, policymakers, and support organizations make more informed decisions about funding, commercialization timing, pilot readiness, and deployment strategy. It turns a vague sense of maturity into a shared language for risk and progress.

## Industry Context

Originally developed by NASA, TRL is now widely used across research, deep tech, public innovation, and commercialization settings. For incubators and accelerators working with technical ventures, it can be especially helpful when a startup's challenge is not just business growth but proving that the underlying technology is actually ready for real-world adoption.
